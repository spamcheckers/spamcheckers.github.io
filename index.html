Layer 01 · Syntax & format

Layer 02 · Disposable domains

Throwaway inboxes, gone

A local database of 100,000+ disposable domains — refreshed on a schedule — stops burner emails cold.

  • 100,000+ throwaway domains on file
  • Auto-refreshed on a schedule

Layer 03 · MX / DNS records

Does the mail server exist?

Live MX and DNS checks confirm the domain can actually receive mail. No records, no entry.

  • Live MX record lookup
  • A / AAAA fallback checks
  • Rejects domains that can't receive mail
Layer 04 · SMTP mailbox probe

Is the mailbox real?

A cautious SMTP probe verifies deliverability for major providers — without ever sending an email.

  • RCPT probe — never sends a message
  • Aware of Gmail, Outlook & more
  • Results cached to stay fast

Questions, answered

Frequently asked questions

Does it stop WordPress contact form spam?

Yes. Email Spam Shield hooks into every major form plugin and scores each submission across seven layers, blocking or flagging spam before it reaches your inbox or database.

Which form plugins are supported?

WooCommerce, Contact Form 7, WPForms, Ninja Forms, Fluent Forms and Gravity Forms — plus native WordPress comments and user registration.

Does it block disposable or temporary email addresses?

Yes. A local database of 100,000+ disposable domains is updated on a schedule and blocks burner inboxes instantly.

Does any of my data leave the server?

No. Every check runs on your own server with no external accounts or API keys, so no visitor data is ever sent to the cloud.

Can I block submissions by country?

Yes. Country blocking resolves locally from a bundled MaxMind or DB-IP database that auto-updates — no per-visit API calls required.
